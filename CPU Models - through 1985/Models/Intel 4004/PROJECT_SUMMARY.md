# Intel 4004 - Project Summary

## Overview
THE FIRST COMMERCIAL MICROPROCESSOR EVER (November 15, 1971).

## Key Specifications
- **Transistors:** 2,300
- **Clock:** 740 kHz
- **Width:** 4 bits
- **IPC:** ~0.09

## Historical Significance
- First complete CPU on a single chip
- Designed for Busicom calculators
- Intel acquired rights for $60,000
- Launched the microprocessor revolution

## Designers
Federico Faggin, Ted Hoff, Stan Mazor, Masatoshi Shima

## Legacy
The 4004 proved microprocessors were possible, enabling:
- Personal computers
- Smartphones
- Internet of Things
- Modern civilization

## Lesson Learned
Revolutionary products open doors. The 4004 wasn't powerful or fast - it was **possible**.
